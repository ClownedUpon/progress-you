Progress You is a personal productivity notebook for people who want one place to manage their time, tasks, and notes — without the complexity of enterprise tools or the limitations of a basic to-do app.

What it does:

Today — Your daily dashboard. Shows your schedule for the day alongside mini task boards for each active section. An Upcoming digest surfaces any time-sensitive tasks with due dates, sorted into three buckets: overdue, this week, and within a month — so nothing with a deadline gets buried.

Timetable — A weekly planner with colour-coded time blocks, one column per day. You assign blocks to sections of your life (work, thesis, personal projects, etc.) or mark them as breaks. Copy last week's schedule in one click.

Boards — A kanban board per section with three columns: Backlog, This Week, and Done. Drag tasks between columns, set optional due dates, add notes, and use spacer dividers to organise long lists. Completed tasks are archived to the monthly log.

Notes — A rich-text notebook per section. Notes are organised in a collapsible tree — you can nest sub-notes under any note, like folders. Each note has a full editor with font, size, colour, bold/italic/underline, and bullet lists.

Log — A monthly archive of everything you've completed, grouped by section.

Stats — A weekly summary: hours scheduled, tasks active and completed, time allocation by section, and completion rate.

Quick Capture — A keyboard shortcut (Ctrl+Space) that opens a focused input from anywhere in the app, so you can write down a task or note instantly without navigating away from what you're doing.

How it's built:

A lightweight desktop app for Windows. All data is stored locally on your machine — nothing is sent to any server. Export your entire notebook as a JSON backup at any time, and import it back if you reinstall or move machines. Sections, colours, and labels are fully customisable.
